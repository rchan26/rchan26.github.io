Ryan Chan

Senior Researcher in Machine Learning for Environmental Forecasting

GitHub LinkedIn Google Scholar CV Email

I’m a senior research associate focusing on end-to-end machine-learning weather forecasting. In particular, I’m working on Project Cumulus, an initiative to develop bespoke data-driven weather subseasonal forecasting systems tailored for farming communities in West Africa.

I have a background in statistics, machine learning and software engineering. I hold a PhD in Statistics from the University of Warwick, supervised by Professor Gareth Roberts and Professor Murray Pollock. In my PhD, I focused on developing Monte Carlo methods for combining distributed statistical analyses (Fusion). I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of Leeds, where I was awarded a first class honours MMath Mathematics degree and received the Royal Statistical Society Prize.

Research & Projects

I am interested in applying machine learning to environment and sustainability problems and currently focusing on end-to-end data-driven weather forecasting.

Current

  • Project Cumulus — Data-driven forecasting systems to support farming communities in West Africa. Leading development of observationally driven models for global sub-seasonal forecasting. [Press release]

Previous

  • FastNet: AI for Numerical Weather Prediction — A Graph Neural Network for medium-range weather forecasting, developed in collaboration between the Met Office and The Alan Turing Institute. [Technical report] [Pre-print]
  • Project t0 — Lean language models using knowledge retrieval and fine-tuning for reasoning and test-time scaling. [Blog] [Technical report] [Code]
  • prompto — Open source software for reproducible experiments on LLMs at scale, supporting self-hosted and API-based models. [NAACL 2025]
  • Rough Paths — Software and applications of rough path theory and path signatures for complex data streams, with applications in NLP.
    • nlpsig: Python library for constructing paths of transformer embeddings.
    • sig-networks: PyTorch library for signature neural networks for longitudinal NLP. [EACL 2024]
    • SigMahaKNN: Anomaly detection on multivariate streams using path signatures. [Paper]
  • IceNet — Probabilistic deep learning sea ice forecasting system led by British Antarctic Survey and The Alan Turing Institute. [Nature Communications] Nominated for the Arctic Circle Frederik Paulsen Award.

Miscellaneous

  • Maya — Multilingual multimodal model based on Cohere’s Aya and LLaVA, part of C4AI’s Expedition Aya 2024. Won the Most Promising Award. [CVPR 2025 Workshop]
  • Reginald — LLM chatbot for The Alan Turing Institute using RAG and llama-index, deployed on the Turing’s Slack workspace.
  • AIrsenal & AIrgentina — Machine learning for Fantasy Premier League and World Cup prediction. AIrgentina came 6th in @Futbolmetrix1’s WC2022 contest, outperforming FiveThirtyEight, Opta, and Betfair. [Blog] [New Scientist]
  • Podcast recommendation systems — Topic modelling and collaborative filtering for podcast discovery, with Entale at ATI Data Study Group (April 2021). [Report]
  • Bayesian Sports Modelling — Bayesian hierarchical models for football match prediction (masters project). [Code]
